# Methodology

## Project Objective

The Global Silver & Gold Producers Analysis project was created to build a structured dataset and analytical framework for evaluating publicly traded precious metals producers.

The project focuses on silver and gold mining companies while integrating production, financial, geographic, and market capitalization data to support comparative analysis, dashboard development, and future SQL database implementation.

## Data Sources

Data is collected from publicly available sources, including:

- Annual Reports
- Quarterly Reports
- Investor Presentations
- Corporate Websites
- Regulatory Filings
- Stock Exchange Filings

Company-reported figures are prioritized whenever available.

## Company Selection

Companies are included based on one or more of the following criteria:

- Significant silver production
- Significant gold production
- Precious metals-focused operations
- Public market listing
- Availability of reliable reporting data

The project primarily tracks producing mining companies rather than exploration-stage companies.

## Data Collection Process

Data is manually collected, reviewed, and organized into the following categories:

- Company Information
- Geographic Information
- Production Metrics
- Financial Metrics
- Market Capitalization Metrics

Each company is assigned a unique CompanyID which serves as the primary identifier across all project datasets.

## Production Data

Production metrics are sourced from company reporting whenever possible.

Tracked metrics may include:

- Silver Production
- Gold Production
- Copper Production
- Lead Production
- Zinc Production
- Other By-Product Metals

Units and reporting periods are standardized where practical to improve comparability.

## Financial Data

Financial metrics are collected from publicly reported company disclosures and financial statements.

Tracked metrics may include:

- Revenue
- Net Income
- Operating Metrics
- Additional Financial Indicators

Values are maintained in the format documented within the dataset.

## Geographic Data

Geographic information is maintained to provide operational and jurisdictional context.

Tracked information may include:

- Headquarters Location
- Primary Operating Countries
- Geographic Regions
- Mine Locations

## Market Capitalization Data

Market capitalization data is collected to provide valuation context for each producer.

Tracked metrics may include:

- Market Capitalization
- Relative Company Size
- Valuation Comparisons

Market capitalization figures reflect the reporting dates documented within the dataset.

## Quality Control

Data is reviewed for:

- Duplicate Records
- Missing Values
- Reporting Errors
- Unit Consistency
- Cross-Sheet Consistency

Significant revisions are documented in the project changelog.

## Current Development Status

The project remains under active development.

Current priorities include:

- Dataset Expansion
- Data Standardization
- Documentation Improvements
- SQL Database Preparation
- Dashboard Enhancement

## Limitations

The project relies on publicly available company reporting and market data.

Differences in reporting standards, reporting periods, and disclosure practices may affect direct comparisons between companies.

Users should reference original company disclosures when conducting investment, academic, or professional research.
